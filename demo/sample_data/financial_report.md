# Q4 2025 Financial Report - Acme Corp

## Executive Summary

Acme Corporation reported strong Q4 2025 results, with total revenue reaching $847 million, a 23% increase year-over-year. Operating income grew to $198 million, reflecting improved operational efficiency and successful cost optimization initiatives across all business units.

## Revenue Breakdown

### Enterprise Software Division
Revenue: $412 million (up 28% YoY)
- Cloud subscription revenue grew 45% to $267 million
- On-premise license revenue declined 12% to $145 million
- Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR) reached $1.1 billion
- Net Revenue Retention Rate: 118%

### Professional Services Division
Revenue: $235 million (up 15% YoY)
- Implementation services: $142 million
- Consulting and advisory: $93 million
- Average project duration decreased from 8.2 to 6.7 months

### Data Analytics Division
Revenue: $200 million (up 31% YoY)
- Platform licensing: $128 million
- Managed analytics services: $72 million
- Customer count grew to 1,247 enterprise accounts

## Key Metrics

| Metric | Q4 2025 | Q4 2024 | Change |

| Total Revenue | $847M | $688M | +23% |
| Gross Margin | 72.4% | 68.1% | +4.3pp |
| Operating Margin | 23.4% | 19.8% | +3.6pp |
| Free Cash Flow | $167M | $112M | +49% |
| Headcount | 12,847 | 11,203 | +14.7% |

## Outlook

Management projects Q1 2026 revenue between $870-890 million. Full year 2026 guidance is $3.7-3.8 billion with operating margins expanding to 25-26%. Key growth drivers include the recently launched AI Analytics Suite and expansion into the Asia-Pacific market.

## Risk Factors

- Increasing competition in the cloud analytics space from major tech providers
- Foreign currency headwinds expected to impact international revenue by 2-3%
- Ongoing investment in AI capabilities may temporarily pressure margins in H1 2026
- Customer concentration: top 10 customers represent 18% of total revenue
