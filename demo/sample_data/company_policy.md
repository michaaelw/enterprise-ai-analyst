# Acme Corporation - Data Governance Policy

## Policy Number: DG-2025-001
## Effective Date: September 1, 2025
## Last Reviewed: December 15, 2025

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes the framework for managing, protecting, and utilizing data assets across Acme Corporation. It applies to all employees, contractors, and third-party partners who access company data.

## 2. Data Classification

All data must be classified into one of four categories:

| Classification | Description | Examples |
|---------------|-------------|----------|
| Public | Information approved for public release | Marketing materials, press releases |
| Internal | General business information | Meeting notes, project plans |
| Confidential | Sensitive business data | Financial reports, customer data, contracts |
| Restricted | Highly sensitive data | PII, trade secrets, security credentials |

## 3. Data Handling Requirements

### 3.1 Access Control
- Access granted on a need-to-know basis following the principle of least privilege
- All access to Confidential and Restricted data requires manager approval
- Quarterly access reviews mandatory for all data systems

### 3.2 Data Retention
- Public data: No retention limit
- Internal data: 5 years after creation
- Confidential data: 7 years after creation or as required by regulation
- Restricted data: As defined by specific regulatory requirements

### 3.3 Data Transfer
- Confidential and Restricted data must be encrypted during transfer
- External data sharing requires Data Protection Officer approval
- Cross-border transfers must comply with applicable data protection laws

## 4. Incident Response

Data breaches or suspected breaches must be reported within 24 hours to:
- IT Security Team: security@acme-corp.example
- Data Protection Officer: dpo@acme-corp.example

## 5. Compliance

Violation of this policy may result in disciplinary action. All employees must complete annual data governance training.
